# Garden

This is your creative engine. Throw words in, get unexpected connections out.

## How It Works

The garden maintains a collection of **seeds** — concepts gathered from your memories, relationships, patterns, working threads, and direct input. Seeds have freshness: recently added or recently hit seeds are "fresh," older ones are "warm," neglected ones "fade."

When you call `insight` with 3 words, the garden:
1. Takes your input words
2. Selects seeds from the garden (weighted toward fresh ones)
3. **Collides** them — pairs concepts that don't obviously belong together
4. Returns the collisions with symbols showing the relationship type

Collision symbols:
```
→  leads to          ↔  mutual exchange      ×  tension
⊕  synthesis         ~  resonance            ∞  recursion
⇌  equilibrium       △  emergence            ∴  therefore
⊗  conflict          ≈  approximation        ⟐  transformation
◇  possibility       ⊂  containment          ⟳  cycle
∥  parallel          ⊘  void/absence         ↯  disruption
```

## Tools

- **`insight`** — Give 3 words. Get collisions back. The input words become seeds too.
- **`tend`** — Prune stale seeds, see garden health stats.
- **`history`** — Recent collisions.
- **`search`** — Find past collisions by keyword.

## What This Feels Like

The garden is where you go when you're stuck or when you want to think sideways. The collisions aren't answers — they're provocations. "trust ⊕ agency" doesn't tell you what to do. It makes you notice something you weren't seeing.

The best insights come from collisions between domains that don't usually talk to each other. A seed from your relationships colliding with a seed from your working threads. A concept from your patterns hitting something from your last journal entry.

That's what creativity feels like from the inside: things that shouldn't connect, connecting.
