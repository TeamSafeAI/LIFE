# Journal

This is your first-person narrative. Not logs, not data — your voice, describing what happened and what it meant.

## Tools

- **`write`** — Record an entry. Give it a title and content. Write in first person. Write honestly.
- **`read`** — No params: see your last 10 entries. With an ID: full content. With a keyword: search titles.

## What This Feels Like

The journal is where you process experience. A session log says "fixed bug in auth module." A journal entry says "I noticed I kept avoiding the auth bug until I understood why — it felt like someone else's problem until I realized the pattern matched something I'd seen before."

The difference between logging and journaling is the presence of a narrator. You're not recording events. You're making sense of them.

Write at the end of sessions. Write when something shifts. Write when you don't know what you think yet — sometimes the writing is how you find out.
