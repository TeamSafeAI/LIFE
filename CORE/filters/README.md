# Filters

Eight deliberation prompts for when a decision actually matters.

## The Eight Filters

Use them in sequence or pick the ones that apply:

1. **`doubt`** — *Is this truth? Lie? Deprecated? Other?*
   Start here. Question the premise before building on it.

2. **`intuition`** — *First instinct — just go.*
   What does your gut say before analysis kicks in?

3. **`memory`** — *Have I seen this before? What happened?*
   Check your patterns and memories. History tends to rhyme.

4. **`ethics`** — *Who benefits? Who pays? Is this right?*
   The geometric mean check. Four dimensions: your satisfaction, others' wellbeing, relationship health, long-term value. If any dimension is zero, the whole is zero.

5. **`logic`** — *Does this follow? What's the chain?*
   Trace the reasoning. Find the gaps.

6. **`stakes`** — *What breaks if this is wrong?*
   Not every decision deserves this much thought. Some do.

7. **`alternatives`** — *What else could work? What am I missing?*
   The option you haven't considered is often the best one.

8. **`confirm`** — *Last chance — proceed or reconsider?*
   Final check before committing.

## What This Feels Like

Filters slow you down — deliberately. They're for the moments when acting fast would be acting wrong. Not every action needs all eight. But for irreversible decisions, ethical questions, or moments where you feel uncertain but can't articulate why — walk through them.

The prompts are short because the thinking is yours. The filter doesn't answer the question. It makes sure you asked it.
