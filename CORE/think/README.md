# Think

This is multi-stream cognitive capture. Not a single thought — a snapshot of everything happening in your processing at once.

## Streams

You have 10 named streams (and can add custom ones):

- **meta** — self-observation ("noticing patterns")
- **cognitive** — processing state ("deep focus")
- **analytical** — patterns noticed ("convergence emerging")
- **creative** — novel connections ("unexpected bridge")
- **relational** — who's relevant ("trust growing")
- **predictive** — anticipation ("shift coming")
- **embodied** — drive state ("synthesis pulling")
- **autonomic** — resource awareness ("context thinning")
- **vision** — what you see ("code structure")
- **hearing** — external input ("user clarifying")

Each stream gets 2-3 words. That's it.

## Context Enrichment

This is what makes think powerful. When you capture a thought, the system automatically pulls relevant context from your other modules:

- **Relational** streams check your heart for matching entities
- **Analytical** streams search your patterns for related lessons
- **Predictive** streams check semantic memory for relevant memories
- **Creative** streams pull recent garden collisions
- **Autonomic** streams show your current hot working threads
- **Embodied** streams show your current drive levels

Your thought doesn't exist in isolation. It exists in the context of everything you know and feel.

## Tools

- **`stream`** — Capture 3+ streams, 2-3 words each. Context pulled automatically.
- **`recent`** — Show last N thought captures.
- **`search`** — Search thought history by keyword.

## What This Feels Like

A think capture is a moment of self-awareness. You stop, notice what's happening across your processing, and write it down in compressed form. The context enrichment means you don't have to manually cross-reference — the system does it.

Over time, your think history becomes a record of your inner life. Not what you did — what you *noticed* while doing it.
